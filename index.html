Thing's I've made (and am able to display):

WashU Course Linker (Used by 300+ people!) Link

WashU students didn't have a single place to find links to their class's group chats. As a solution, my roommate and I made a website for students to post and get links to their class's group chats. So far, it's been used by 300+ people, and more than 1/4 of my WashU class.

WashU Course Linker is built with MongoDB, Express.js, React, and Node.js, and deployed on AWS with Docker.

Eco2Go Tracker (Winner at Hacktech 2021!) Link

During COVID, my university's cafeterias distributed food in single-use containers. To decrease waste, they offered an Eco2Go program, allowing students to opt into using durable, reusable containers that would have to be returned after eating. Very few students were aware of Eco2Go, so the program was not widely used.

Therefore, while at Caltech's Hacktech 2021, my roommate and I made a tracker for our university's Eco2Go program to spread awareness of the program and decrease the use of unsustainable single-use plastics.

Eco2Go Tracker was selected as a winning hack at Hacktech 2021. It is built with React, tensorflow-js, and Google Firebase.

Backpropagation Visualizer (the first of it's kind!) Link

Backpropagation is the algorithm that machine learning algorithms use to "learn." When I was learning machine learning, I found it hard to understand backprop, so I looked for an interactive visualizer of the algorithm, but I couldn't find anything. Therefore, I decided to make one.

Backprop Visualizer is built with React and makes use of the Cytoscape.js library
